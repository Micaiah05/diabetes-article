    • Overview of diabetes

    Type 1 diabetes is a genetic disease caused by high blood sugar. Let's say that you just ate some food. This food that you consumed contains glucose, which is the body's primary energy source. Normally, if the body detects high glucose levels in the blood, the pancreas secretes insulin, a hormone that helps regulate glucose levels so it doesnt get as high. In other words, insulin acts like a "key" to open up cells so that they can take in the excess glucose as energy, allowing not much glucose to remain in the bloodstream. If you have type 1 diabetes, your pancreas cannot secrete insulin, therefore allowing glucose in the blood to spike. However, although this may seem overwhelming and scary, there is good news! Type 1 diabetes can be managed by regular insulin injections.

    Conclusion Questions

    > How does life with type 1 diabetes compare to life with type 2 diabetes? Mention at least three specific comparisons.

  • Type 1 diabetics are likely to experience mood swings while type 2 diabetics may experience numbness in their feet.
  • Type 1 diabetes develops over several weeks of increased thirst and urination, while type 2 diabetes develops over several years of symptoms.
  • A regulation of symptoms for type 1 diabetes could be a pancreas islet transplant, while gastric bypass can be used to regulate symptoms for type 2 diabetics.

    • > When a person is diagnosed with diabetes, what changes would one have to make in lifestyle and diet?

    Keep away from sugary foods, quit smoking, and keep track of blood sugar.

    > Describe the importance of checking blood sugar for a diabetic.

    Because high blood sugar can lead to many complications, like ketoacidosis, which the body breaks down fat too fast. This can cause blood to be acidic and could contribute towards nausea and abdominal pain.
