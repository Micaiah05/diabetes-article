    • Overview of diabetes

    Type 1 diabetes is a genetic disease caused by high blood sugar. Let's say that you just ate some food. This food that you consumed contains glucose, which is the body's primary energy source. Normally, if the body detects high glucose levels in the blood, the pancreas secretes insulin, a hormone that helps regulate glucose levels so it doesnt get as high. In other words, insulin acts like a "key" to open up cells so that they can take in the excess glucose as energy, allowing not much glucose to remain in the bloodstream. If you have type 1 diabetes, your pancreas cannot secrete insulin, therefore allowing glucose in the blood to spike. However, although this may seem overwhelming and scary, there is good news! Type 1 diabetes can be managed by regular insulin injections.

    Recommendations of a diabetic diet

    > What foods should I limit?

  • It is advised to limit food intake of anything "white", for example white bread, white pastas, white rice, white tortillas, an so on.
  • Anything with high fat or added sugar content should be limited or avoided.

    • > What foods are the best for me?

  • Leafy green vegetables, whole grain products, fatty fish, and nuts & seeds are just a few of the foods that are good for a diabetic diet.
  • Eat a lot of fruits and veggies - the more colorful the better.
  • Foods high in fiber, such as chia seed or broccoli will be great.

    Blood sugar monitoring and management

    Blood sugar monitoring is one of the ways to keep track of your blood sugar levels. Ways to do this for type 1 diabetics include:

  • A CGM (or continuous glucose monitor) is a sensor that is inserted into your skin that periodically measures blood sugar levels, with increments of a few minutes each time.
  • A blood sugar meter uses blood from your fingertips to measure your blood sugar levels.

    • These types of blood sugar tests can be conducted at home. The blood sugar meter testing works by measuring your blood sugar level before and after you eat or exercise. For example, let's say that you measure your blood sugar to be 85mg/dl before eating, and after you eat your blood sugar is measured at 275mg/dl, it can be concluded that whatever you ate isn't doing a good job of keeping your blood sugar low, and therefore you should exclude that particular food from your diet. To put it in other words, the role of blood sugar monitoring and management is to regulate diabetes by changing your diet according to your blood sugar levels. You will be able to learn the effects of certain foods or activities on blood sugar.

    Exercise, lifestyle, and diabetes

    > Recommendations for exercise

  • Adults with type 1 diabetes should get at least 150 minutes of aerobic exercise a week (5 days a week, 30 min daily) with intensity ranging from moderate to vigorous.
  • Not only will type 1 diabetics experience high blood sugar, they may also experience low blood sugar (hypoglycemia). To decrease a big drop in blood sugar while exercising, some studies show that incorporating sprints for a short period of time in regular intervals while working out will help.
  • Resistance activities would also help lessen the drop in blood sugar, such as lifting weights.

    • > Recommendations for lifestyle

  • Eat healthy. Get enough of vegetables, fruits, and whole grains and limit foods that are high in fat and/or sugar.
  • Stop smoking. Smoking can make it easier to acquire complications that come from diabetes, which include heart disease, nerve damage, and foot problems.
  • Learn how to manage stress. Stress can elevate blood sugar levels and could make you forget about treating your diabetes.

    • Staying fit and healthy can help regulate diabetes. Everytime you exercise your heart becomes healthier and stronger, preventing complications such as heart disease. Also, after exercise, your body isn't as sensitive to insulin; in other words the body will not need much insulin to regulate blood sugar so the spike in blood sugar won't be as severe. On top of that, incorporating blood sugar monitoring discussed previously, exercising can make you learn and understand what effects certain exercises have on blood sugar levels.

    Professionals that can help you

  • Nutritionist or Dietitian - Are individuals who implement diet plans for certain individuals and are experts in food nutrition. They could help you by implementing a special diet that suits you the best, because they know all about nutrients and their effects.
  • Clinical Researcher - They research diseases and cures for them. They may also find cures for new diseases as well. Clinical researchers can help you both in the short and long run; you would be able to learn the best way to regulate diabetes from them, and maybe they will eventually find a cure for diabetes.
  • Certified Diabetes Educator - They possess a vast amount of knowledge about diabetes, from treating it to preventing it. You could learn more about the causes of diabetes and how to prevent it from them. Since they know a lot about diabetes, they could give you the best treatment or regulation possible, just like a clinical researcher.

    > How does life with type 1 diabetes compare to life with type 2 diabetes? Mention at least three specific comparisons.

    Type 1 diabetics are likely to experience mood swings while type 2 diabetics may experience numbness in their feet. Type 1 diabetes develops over several weeks of increased thirst and urination, while type 2 diabetes develops over several years of symptoms. A regulation of symptoms for type 1 diabetes could be a pancreas islet transplant, while gastric bypass can be used to regulate symptoms for type 2 diabetics.

    > When a person is diagnosed with diabetes, what changes would one have to make in lifestyle and diet?

    Keep away from sugary foods, quit smoking, and keep track of blood sugar.

    > Describe the importance of checking blood sugar for a diabetic.

    Because high blood sugar can lead to many complications, like ketoacidosis, which the body breaks down fat too fast. This can cause blood to be acidic and could contribute towards nausea and abdominal pain.
